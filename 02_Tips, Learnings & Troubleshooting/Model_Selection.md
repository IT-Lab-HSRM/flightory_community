# Model Selection & General Questions

### Is the Stallion/VTOL suitable for a first build?
**Community consensus: these are experienced/expert builds.**

> "Hmm, this might not be the best thing to do for your first go around with planes and VTOLs. These are really considered experienced/expert builds. There are a lot of YouTube videos out there that explain the basics of RC plane builds."

**Suggested path for beginners:**
1. Learn basics with a foam trainer
2. Build a simpler fixed wing first
3. Progress to a Flightory model when comfortable with electronics and firmware

---

### Moose vs Stallion — launch and handling (fb)
**Question:** Is the Moose easier to hand-launch than the Stallion?

**Answer:** Yes. The Stallion's shape makes it hard to grip during a throw — two community members reported mishaps from it slipping. The Moose (twin-tractor design) is much easier to hold and launch. Generally: only the Pico Talon, Plank, and Lark are light enough for a one-handed throw; larger models need two hands.

### What budget do I need?
**No definitive figure given in the group**, but based on components discussed:
- Files: varies by model (check flightory.com)
- Filament: 2–4 kg of LW-PLA/ASA (~€25–40/kg for quality brands)
- Electronics (FC, ESC, motors, servos, GPS, receiver, VTX): €150–400 depending on spec
- Battery: €40–100
- Misc (CF tubes, hardware, glue, etc.): €20–50

Total estimate: **€300–600+** for a first complete VTOL build.

---

### Stallion VTOL — flight time expectations
**Realistic expectations from community flyers:**
- Hover-heavy flying: **10–15 minutes**
- Efficient forward flight (cruise-focused): **30–45 minutes**
- Optimised long-range build: **1–2 hours**
